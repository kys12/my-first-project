Down Syndrome


  • Typical craniofacial appearance
    • Round face and flat nasal bridge
    • Epicanthic folds (see image) - fold of skin running across inner edge of palpebral fissure
    • Brushfield spots in iris (pigmented spots)
    • Small ears, small mouth, protruding tongue
    • Flat occiput and third fontanelle
  • Other anomalies
    • Short neck, Single palmar creases, wide 'sandal' gap between toes
    • Hypotonia, Congenital heart defect, Duodenal atresia
  • Later medical problems
    • Delayed motor milestones, severe learning difficulties
    • Small stature, Hearing impairment (secretory otitis media), Visual impairment (cataracts, squints, myopia), Increased risk of hypothyroidism, coeliac disease and leukaemia/tumours, Epilepsy, Alzheimeri's disease
